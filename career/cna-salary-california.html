CNA Salary in California


How much does a Nursing Assistant make in California?

Explore CNA Earnings, Pay Factors, and Career Insights

Certified Nurse Assistants (CNAs) play a critical role in healthcare, providing essential patient care under the supervision of licensed nurses. Understanding the salary landscape for CNAs in California can help you make informed career decisions.

Average CNA Salary

On average, CNAs in California earn between $41,000 - $45,000 annually (approximately $20/hour). Entry-level positions may start lower, while experienced CNAs or those working in high-demand regions can earn significantly more.

Hourly vs Annual Pay

  • Hourly: $18 - $20/hour
  • Annual: $41,000 - $45,000

Factors Influencing CNA Pay

  • Location: CNAs in areas with higher living costs, like San Francisco or the Bay Area, typically earn more.
  • Experience: Entry-level CNAs start lower ($33k/year), while seasoned professionals earn more ($46k+).
  • Employer Type: Hospitals and specialized care centers often offer higher wages than smaller nursing facilities.
  • Shift Differentials: Night, weekend, and holiday shifts usually come with additional pay.

Examples of Pay Ranges

Entry-Level: $16 - $22/hour (~$33,000 - $46,000/year)
Experienced / Top-Paying Locations: $24 - $26+/hour (~$50,000+/year)

Key Takeaways

The salary for CNAs varies widely depending on location, experience, facility type, and shift. To maximize earnings, research opportunities in top-paying cities like San Francisco, San Jose, or Santa Clara, and consider facilities that offer shift differentials or specialized care roles.
